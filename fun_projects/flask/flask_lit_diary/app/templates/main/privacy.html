1. Data We Collect

When you register and use Literary Diary, we store the following personal data:

  • Account information — your email address, display name, and a hashed (PBKDF2-SHA256) copy of your password. Your plain-text password is never stored.
  • Notes — title, subtitle, body, book title, date, cover image URL, and visibility setting (public or private).
  • Comments — the text of comments you post on notes.

We do not store contact form submissions locally. Messages sent through the contact form are forwarded via SendGrid (see Section 3) and are not retained in our database.

2. Cookies

We set a single session cookie when you log in. It keeps you authenticated while you browse. It is deleted when your session ends.

We use no analytics cookies, advertising cookies, or third-party tracking cookies.

3. Third-Party Processors

Using the site causes your browser to contact several external services. Each receives limited technical data (typically your IP address and browser User-Agent):

Service When contacted Data sent
Gravatar Pages that show comments An MD5 hash of your email address, used to load your avatar
SendGrid When you submit the contact form Your name, email address, and message
Google Fonts CDN Every page load IP address, browser User-Agent, timestamp
Font Awesome CDN Every page load IP address, browser User-Agent, timestamp
jsDelivr (Bootstrap JS) Every page load IP address, browser User-Agent, timestamp
Open Library API When you search for a book while writing a note Your search query

4. Your Rights

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) you have the following rights over your personal data:

  • Right of access and portability (Art. 15, 20) — You can download a full copy of your data (profile, notes, and comments) in JSON format at any time via the data export endpoint.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — You can edit or update any note you have written from within the app.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) — You can permanently delete your account, including all your notes and comments, from the account menu (DELETE ACCOUNT). This action is irreversible.
  • Right to restrict processing or object (Art. 18, 21) — You can set any note to private at any time, limiting its visibility to yourself only. For broader restrictions or objections, contact us using the details below.

5. Contact

For any privacy-related requests not covered by the self-service options above, please use the contact form.
